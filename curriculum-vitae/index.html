Art

After studying film-based photography as part of my VCE studies, I was selected to exhibit one of my folio pieces in the VCE Top Arts exhibition at the National Gallery of Victoria in 2000. This inspired me to continue indulging my passion for photography throughout my adult years, frequently building on my own success to assist with the education and development of other aspiring photographers through weekend workshops for senior secondary school students and amateur enthusiasts at a local photography studio.

More recently, I was able to employ my digital photography skills in my own part-time business, assisting aspiring models with the development of their professional folios, whilst also selling my own High Dynamic Range (HDR) landscape and nature photography prints online.

Today my interest in digital art has evolved beyond photography, with most of my current creative energy focused on exploring the boundless world of photo-realistic, computer generated (CG) art that has been opened up to the masses through the open-source 3D modelling software, Blender.

Aviation

Whilst completing my secondary school studies, I learned to fly light, fixed-wing aircraft, and was subsequently awarded an Australian Private Pilot's License (PPL) before I was even allowed to drive a car!

I continued my flight training alongside my university studies, and went on to acquire an Australian Commercial Pilot's License (CPL), which allowed me to accrue several hundred hours experience as pilot-in-command of both aerial work and charter flights for a local operator.

Although medical issues prevented me from pursuing ongoing flight training up to Air Transport Pilot's License (ATPL) level, my passion for aviation remains close to my heart and mind to this day.

Basketball

Having played basketball throughout my childhood years, refereeing was a natural first job for me at the age of fourteen. I have continued my involvement in officiating to this day, having worked my way up the hierarchy to referee basketball at the elite Victorian state junior championship (VJBL) and state senior championship (BigV) levels.

My involvement in officiating does not cease when the final siren sounds: off the court, I have been actively involved in the management and development of referees at all levels of the game through my roles as President of my local referees association, as a referee supervisor for state junior championship competitions, and as a referee coach at local domestic competitions.
